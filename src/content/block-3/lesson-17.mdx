Dynamic vocabulary (*plummet/soar*) is **forbidden** here. Nothing moves.
You need **Comparative Structures**.

* **X is twice as high as Y.**
* **X is three times the size of Y.**
* **X amounts to 50%, whereas Y accounts for only 10%.**
* **X is the dominant category.**

**Grouping Strategy:**
Do not list every number. Group them:

* *Group 1:* The Giants (The biggest slices/numbers).
* *Group 2:* The Dwarfs (The smallest numbers).

### Drill 2: "Making Up" the Whole

You need varied verbs to say "X is 50%".

* *constitute / comprise / account for / make up / represent*

**Task:** Rewrite "Cars are 40% of the total."

1. Cars [account for|hint:Verb] 40% of the total.
2. Cars [constitute|hint:Formal verb] a significant portion of the total (40%).
3. The category of cars [makes up|hint:Phrasal verb] 40%.
