**Text Segment:** *The Philosophy of Art.*

> While classical aesthetics focused on the mimicry of nature, modernism abruptly severed this tie. It posited that art should not reflect reality but rather reconstruct it through the artist's subjective lens. Critics of modernism, such as Greenberg, argued that this shift led to a dangerous detachment from the human experience. Conversely, proponents like Adorno suggested that only through abstraction could art truly critique the fractured nature of modern society.

**Questions 1-3: Yes / No / Not Given**

**1. Modernism emphasizes the exact copying of the natural world.**
<Hint>
* *Scan:* "Modernism... abruptly severed this tie [mimicry of nature]".
* *Logic:* Severed = Cut/Stopped.
* *Answer:* **NO**.
</Hint>
**2. Greenberg believed that modernism brought art closer to human emotion.**
<Hint>
* *Scan:* Greenberg -> "dangerous detachment from the human experience".
* *Logic:* Detachment = Moving away.
* *Answer:* **NO**.
</Hint>
**3. Adorno and Greenberg agreed on the role of abstraction.**
<Hint>
* *Scan:* Adorno -> "Conversely" (Contrast word). Greenberg criticized; Adorno was a proponent.
* *Logic:* They disagreed.
* *Answer:* **NO**. (Wait, did the text say they disagreed *specifically* on "abstraction"? Text says Adorno suggested abstraction... Greenberg argued about detachment. The text implies disagreement on the *value* of modernism, but does it explicitly say Greenberg *hated* abstraction? "Conversely" suggests opposing views. Greenberg criticized the *shift*. Adorno supported *abstraction*. It's a contradiction).
* *Correction:* Let's look closer. Greenberg criticized the *shift*. Adorno supported the *abstraction*. One is a critic, one is a proponent. So they do **not** agree. The statement says they "agreed". So the answer is **NO**.
**Wait, let's check the "Not Given" trap.**
Does the text say Greenberg talked about "abstraction"? It says he talked about "this shift". We assume the shift includes abstraction.
*Actually, strictly speaking:* The text contrasts their *views on modernism*. It does not explicitly state Greenberg's specific view on the word "abstraction," only "this shift."
*IELTS Logic:* "Conversely" links Adorno (Pro-Abstraction) against Greenberg (Anti-Shift). The opposition is clear. Answer: **NO**.
</Hint>

**4. Adorno believed art should fix society.**
<Hint>
* *Scan:* Adorno -> "critique the fractured nature".
* *Logic:* "Critique" (Criticize) is NOT "Fix" (Repair).
* *Answer:* **NOT GIVEN**. (He wanted to critique it; the text doesn't say he wanted to *fix* it).
</Hint>
