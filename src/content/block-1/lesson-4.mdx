**Topic:** *Coherence & Cohesion (CC) - Moving beyond "Firstly, Secondly"*.
**Exam Relevance:** Band 6 students use "mechanical linkers" (*First, Second, Furthermore, Also*) at the start of every sentence. Band 8 students use **Referencing** and **Mid-sentence linkers**.

## 1. üìò Deep Theory: The "This + Noun" Strategy

The most powerful tool for cohesion is **Referencing**. Instead of repeating a word or using a basic pronoun ("it"), use a demonstrative pronoun + a summary noun.

* **Bad (Repetitive):** "Pollution is rising. Pollution is bad."
* **Better (Band 6):** "Pollution is rising. **It** is bad."
* **Band 8 (Referencing):** "Pollution is rising. **This alarming trend** is having negative consequences."

**Common "Summary Nouns" for Referencing:**

* *This trend / This development / This phenomenon* (for changes).
* *This view / This perspective / This notion* (for opinions).
* *This measure / This approach / This strategy* (for solutions).
* *This issue / This challenge / This obstacle* (for problems).

## 2. üìò Deep Theory: Participle Clauses as Linkers

Instead of starting a new sentence with "As a result...", attach the result to the previous sentence using an **-ing clause**.

* **Band 6:** "The government raised taxes. As a result, consumption fell."
* **Band 8:** "The government raised taxes, **resulting in** a fall in consumption."
* **Band 8:** "The factory closed down, **leaving** hundreds unemployed."
