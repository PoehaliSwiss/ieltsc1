## 1. üìò Deep Theory: The Band 8 Criteria

The difference between Band 6 and Band 8 is not just vocabulary. It is **Development**.

* **Band 6 (The List):** You list many arguments but don't explain them.
* *Example:* "Pollution is bad because it kills animals. Also it makes air dirty. And it causes global warming." (Too many ideas, shallow).


* **Band 8 (The Deep Dive):** You pick **one or two** ideas and extend them fully.
* *Example:* "Pollution poses a severe threat to biodiversity. When toxic waste enters ecosystems, it disrupts the food chain, leading to the extinction of vulnerable species. Consequently, this destabilizes the entire environment." (One idea, fully explained, result given).



### The PEEL Method (Body Paragraphs)

To ensure you get a high Coherence score, every body paragraph must follow this structure:

1. **P - Point:** The Topic Sentence. What is this paragraph about?
2. **E - Explain:** Why is this true? (Use: *This is because...*, *In other words...*)
3. **E - Example:** Give specific evidence. (Use: *For instance...*, *A clear example of this is...*)
4. **L - Link:** Connect back to the question. (Use: *Therefore...*, *As a result...*)

---

## 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è The "Generalization" Trap

Examiners hate over-generalizations.

* **Don't say:** "Everyone knows that..." / "All teenagers are addicted to phones."
* **Do say:** "It is widely acknowledged that..." / "A significant number of teenagers display signs of addiction."
