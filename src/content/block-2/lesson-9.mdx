## 1. ðŸ“˜ Deep Theory: The Binary Choice

You are acting like a lawyer in a court. You have **Evidence (The Text)** and a **Statement (The Question)**.

* **TRUE (YES):** The statement agrees with the information in the text 100%. (Synonyms are used).
* **FALSE (NO):** The statement **contradicts** the text. (Text says "Black", Question says "White").
* **NOT GIVEN:** The information is **missing**. You cannot prove it true, and you cannot prove it false.

**The "Not Given" Trap:**
The text might mention the *topic*, but not the specific *fact* in the question.

* *Text:* "The car is expensive."
* *Statement:* "The car is fast."
* *Verdict:* **NOT GIVEN**. (Expensive cars are usually fast, but the text didn't *say* it. Do not assume!).
