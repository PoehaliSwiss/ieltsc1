**Topic:** *Reading Question Type: Matching Features (Names to Theories)*.
**Exam Relevance:** You have a box of Names (Researchers/Scientists) and a list of Statements (Theories/Findings). You must match who said what.
**The Trap:** The names appear multiple times in the text. You cannot just look at the first mention.

## 1. ðŸ“˜ Deep Theory: The "Box First" Strategy

Do **not** read the text first.

1. **Scan for the Names:** Go to the text and circle/underline *every* instance of the names in the box. (Look for Capital Letters).
2. **Read around the Name:** Read the sentence *before* and *after* the name. Look for "reporting verbs" (*claimed, argued, discovered, noted*).
3. **Match to Statement:** Find the synonym in the statements list.

**NB:** Some names might be used twice. Some might not be used at all (if the instructions don't say "NB: You may use any letter more than once").

**Topic:** *Reading Question Type: Summary Completion (with or without a box)*.
**Exam Relevance:** You must fill in gaps in a summary of the text.
**The Secret Weapon:** **Grammar**. The answer must fit grammatically. If the gap requires a noun, an adjective will be wrong.

## 1. ðŸ“˜ Deep Theory: The Grammar Prediction

Before looking for the answer in the text, analyze the gap.

* "a __________" -> Noun (Singular).
* "are __________" -> Adjective or Participle or Noun (Plural).
* "to __________" -> Verb (Infinitive).

This filters out 50% of the wrong answers (distractors).

## 2. ðŸ› ï¸ Mega Practice: The Grammar Filter

**Summary Text:**

> The ancient city was discovered in 1920. It was famous for its unique system of (1) __________ which supplied water to the inhabitants. Unlike other cities, it did not rely on (2) __________ rivers.

**Text:**

> Excavations revealed an elaborate network of **canals** that irrigated the region. This was crucial because the city was situated in an arid zone, far from any **natural** water sources.

**Gap 1 Analysis:**

* *Grammar:* "system of..." -> Needs a **Noun** (Plural or Uncountable).
* *Scan:* "network of **canals**".
* *Check:* Does "canals" fit? Yes.

**Gap 2 Analysis:**

* *Grammar:* "rely on ... rivers" -> Needs an **Adjective**.
* *Scan:* "far from any **natural** water sources".
* *Check:* Does "natural" fit? Yes.
