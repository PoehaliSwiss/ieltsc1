**Topic:** *Reading Question Types (Matching Headings)*.

**Exam Relevance:** You have 7 headings and 5 paragraphs. You must find the "Main Idea".

**The Trap:** The text contains **Distractors**. Paragraph A mentions "money". Heading iv mentions "financial costs". But is "money" the *main idea* of Paragraph A, or just a detail?

## 1. üìò Deep Theory: Topic Sentence Identification

The **Main Idea** covers the *entire* paragraph. A **Detail** covers only one sentence.

**Strategy:**

1. Read the Headings first. Underline keywords.
2. Read the **first sentence** of the Paragraph. (80% of the time, the answer is here).
3. If not, read the **last sentence**.
4. Do **not** match individual words. Match the **meaning**.

# üèõÔ∏è Lesson 11: Listening Section 1 & 2 ‚Äì Precision

**Topic:** *Listening Strategy (Survival & Prediction)*.
**Exam Relevance:**

* **Section 1:** Two speakers (Social). Filling a form. **Trap:** Spelling & Numbers.
* **Section 2:** One speaker (Social). Describing a map or event. **Trap:** Distractors.

## 1. üìò Deep Theory: The "Ghost" Method (Prediction)

You have ~30 seconds before the audio starts. You must **predict** the answer.

* If the gap is: "Total cost: $______", you listen for a **Number**.
* If the gap is: "Name: ______", you listen for **Spelling**.
* If the gap is: "Meeting place: The ______", you listen for a **Noun (Place)**.

**The Correction Trap (Review):**
As learned in Block 3 (previous course structure), beware of: *"It's at 5 PM... oh sorry, no, make that 6 PM."* Always write the **last** value.
